Soba Noodles

Soba noodles are a type of Japanese noodle made from buckwheat flour. They are typically served in a light, savory broth or with a dipping sauce. Soba noodles are known for their nutty flavor and chewy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 200g soba noodles
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Toppings: sliced pork, soft-boiled egg, nori, bamboo shoots

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine vegetable broth, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld together.
  2. While the broth is simmering, cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Once the broth is ready, strain it to remove the solids and return the liquid to the pot. Keep it warm over low heat.
  4. To serve, divide the cooked noodles into bowls and ladle the hot broth over them. Add your desired toppings such as sliced pork, soft-boiled egg, nori, and bamboo shoots. Enjoy your delicious bowl of soba!
